## Why This Blog Exists

If you've landed here, you're probably wondering: *"Who is this person and why should I read their thoughts?"*

Fair question. Here's my pitch in 30 seconds:

I'm a **DevSecOps engineer** by day who can't stop tinkering by night. My brain operates on a simple loop:

1. See something interesting
2. Wonder "how does this work?"
3. Take it apart (physically or digitally)
4. Understand it
5. Make it better (or break it trying)
6. Repeat at 2am

This blog is where I dump my discoveries, document my projects, and occasionally write down thoughts that would otherwise keep me up at night.

## Why "Break to Understand"?

Throughout my journey, I've realized the best way I learn is by breaking things down:

- **Security**: You can't defend what you don't understand. I learned networking by intercepting packets, authentication by finding edge cases, and encryption by... well, let's just say I've made some mistakes.

- **Hardware**: Every failed 3D print taught me more than the successful ones. Every burned component was a lesson in "read the datasheet, dummy."

- **Code**: The debugger is my best friend. I've learned more from `printf("here")` statements than any tutorial.

Breaking things isn't destructive - it's curiosity in action.
